About Me

Currently navigating the tumultuous waters of a Computer Science degree at Tecnológico de Monterrey, my journey has become an intricate dance with the rapidly evolving realm of technology. Amidst the complexities of this academic odyssey, my focus is honed sharply on web development, delving deep into the intricate labyrinth of backend systems. Yet, beyond the confines of the digital landscape, I find myself entangled in the captivating world of robotics, where innovation seamlessly transitions from the ethereal realms of code to the tangible complexities of the physical world. In the midst of this technological whirlwind, passion propels me forward, threading the delicate balance between breakdowns and breakthroughs on this challenging yet exhilarating career path.

Projects

Project 1: Android App Development for Social Organization Support

In my first venture into the world of software development, my team and I embarked on the creation of an Android app aimed at aiding social organizations in their financial endeavors. The app, designed for Frisa and YCO, served as a comprehensive catalog where users could access detailed information about these organizations. As the one responsible for the API and backend development, I delved into the intricacies of MongoDB to bring this app to life. Yet, despite the success of the project, I couldn't help but ponder the broader implications of technology on societal structures and whether our efforts truly made a lasting impact.

Project 2: Robotics Education for Kids in DIF

Amidst my existential musings, I found myself contributing to the community by helping kids in the DIF (Desarrollo Integral de la Familia) learn the wonders of robotics. Together, we explored the realms of circuits, sensors, and basic robot programming to navigate obstacles. While witnessing the excitement in the children's eyes, a nagging question lingered in my mind – are we truly preparing them for a future filled with uncertainties, or are we merely perpetuating the cycle of conformity in education?

Project 3: Teaching Videogame Development to Kids with Axateam Monterrey

In another endeavor, I took on the role of a mentor, guiding young minds in the art of video game development using C# and Unity. Collaborating with the Raspberry Pi Foundation, we aimed to instill creativity and technological prowess in the kids associated with Axateam Monterrey. However, as I shared my knowledge, I couldn't shake off the gnawing doubt about the societal implications of introducing children to a world driven by digital entertainment. Was I inadvertently steering them away from more meaningful pursuits, or was I empowering them to navigate the complexities of the modern technological landscape?
